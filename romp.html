The Problem

NEWCOMERS IN THE CITY ARE STRUGGLE TO INTEGRATE INTO THEIR LOCAL COMMUNITIES AND COMBAT FEELING OF ISOLATION. PEOPLE HAVE NO IDEA HOW TO FIND THE SOCIAL EVENTS TO MEET OTHERS AND IT IS NOT EASY TO FIND SOCIAL EVENTS THE TIME AND WHAT THEY WANT.

The Research

The Goal
HOW do newcomers integrate into their communities and HOW can we better their experience?
Literature Review (AI)
Key Insights:
Implications for UX Design
  1. Platform Integration:
    Our platform uses APIs from Google, Instagram, Facebook, Evently, etc., to collect a comprehensive list of events.
  2. Efficient Data Handling:
    AI sorts and formats events to fit the app’s organization and layout, ensuring clear and intuitive presentation.
  3. Personalized Recommendations:
    An algorithm personalizes event suggestions based on user preferences and behaviors, enhancing user engagement.
  4. Scalability:
    The platform design supports scalability and flexibility to handle growth and varying user needs.
Literature Review (Social Wellbeing)
Key Insights:
Implications for UX Design
  1. Enhancing Social Interactions:
    Design features that facilitate and encourage social interactions to improve user engagement and well-being.
  2. Supporting Vulnerable Groups:
    Develop solutions specifically aimed at supporting groups prone to loneliness, such as the elderly and new mothers.
  3. Promoting Mental Health:
     Incorporate elements that foster community, trust, and cooperation to promote mental health benefits like reduced anxiety and increased self-esteem.
  4. Preventing Loneliness:
    Create platforms and tools that help users build and maintain social connections to mitigate the risks associated with loneliness.
The Solutions
1. Start/ Sign up
Our start/signup prototype ensures users can effortlessly understand the app's concept and smoothly complete the signup process.
This part of the onboarding process lets users inform the AI about the types of events they enjoy, providing a foundation for the algorithm to personalize recommendations.
2. Home/Saved Events
Users can explore nearby events and search for events by category.
Users can view event details, purchase tickets, and book events directly through the app. Additionally, the app allows users to save events they like and easily access their saved events.
3.Schedule
The app now integrates with Google Calendar and features a friendly AI personality. Users can easily browse through all options generated by the AI.
The AI recommends options based on users' interests, making it easy for them to find local events and integrate into the local community.
4.Profile
The profile page lets users view past events they joined and saved events.
Additionally, users can edit their interests to help the AI recommend events tailored to their preferences.

The Ideation

User Personas
Our group created three personas after reviewing the interview data. We got a newcomer in the city, a transfer student, and an elder for the interview. This allowed me to better understand the needs of potential users and define my ideas.
User Flow
Following our brainstorming session, we developed a user flow to structure the mobile app, ensuring it effectively guides users towards achieving their three primary end goals:
  1. Home page
  2. Calendar
  3. Profile page

The Design & Iteration

The Sketch
To overcome a creative block in defining feature functionalities, we began by sketching our ideas. This process allowed us to explore multiple calendar layout possibilities and refine the AI functionalities.
Sketching enabled us to visualize and iterate on the wireframes, ensuring that the final designs were user-friendly, intuitive, and aesthetically pleasing.
Wireframe
Feedback Summary
  1. AI Personality: Interactions feel impersonal due to the AI's lack of personality.
  2. Button Design: Buttons are unclear and unintuitive.
  3. Limited Options: Insufficient options for user flexibility.
  4. Calendar Management: Difficult to manage multiple calendars, especially for Google Calendar users.
  5. Layout Issues: Overall layout needs improvement.
  6. Biography Section: The biography section is too long.
  7. Profile Customization: Customization options are unclear.
  8. Personal Touch: The app lacks a personal feel for users.
1 Version High - Fidelity
  1. Visualize and test multiple calendar layout options and AI functionalities
  2. Develop wireframes prioritizing user-friendliness, intuitiveness, and aesthetic appeal.
  3. Refine button designs for clarity and intuitive use.
  4. Enhance profile customization and shorten the biography section for a more personalized experience.
  5. Enable seamless calendar management with schedule imports from other apps.

The Reflection

User Testing 👩‍💻
Overall, the feedback on the app was positive. Users praised the new feature of sharing schedules with Google Calendar and appreciated its ability to help newcomers find local events and integrate into the community, even when they don't know anyone.
After user testing, some users experienced difficulty with the AI, particularly in terms of customization, providing multiple options, and integrating into their schedules. To address this, we added a customized options onboarding process where users can input their personal interests. Additionally, the AI now has a friendly personality, making it easier for users to browse through all the options it generates.
Lesson 💡
This project taught me the importance of empathy, as I could relate to the user problem of finding local events when moving to a new place, like when I moved to Ann Arbor. This understanding helped me focus on the most crucial needs to address in our solution. Additionally, I gained valuable time management skills due to the short-term nature of the project.
